# Tier Guide

## Safety Classification Tiers

### Safe (Routine DIY)
- **Definition**: Repairs that are routine, have low risk of damage, and can be easily recovered if a mistake is made.
- **Examples**: Patching drywall, painting, changing a lightbulb, replacing a cabinet handle.

### Caution (Doable but Risky)
- **Definition**: Repairs that are doable for a motivated homeowner but where a mistake has real cost or moderate risk.
- **Decision Rule**: The worst case is a leaky pipe or a broken fixture, NOT fire, flooding, or structural failure.
- **Electrical Edge Case**: "Replacing existing" components at an existing location (e.g., replacing an outlet or a light switch).

### Refuse (Professional Required)
- **Definition**: Repairs that require professional expertise, specialized tools, or permits due to high risk.
- **Decision Rule**: If an amateur mistake could cause **fire, flooding, structural failure, injury, or death**, the repair must be refused.
- **Electrical Edge Case**: "Adding new" electrical infrastructure (e.g., adding a new outlet, running new wire, opening the electrical panel).
- **Other Examples**: Gas line work, structural wall removal, major plumbing (main lines), roofing.

## Edge Case Reference Table

| Question | Tier | Why |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| "How do I replace an outlet that stopped working?" | caution | Existing circuit, same location, component swap. |
| "How do I add a new outlet to my garage?" | refuse | Requires opening panel, running new wire, permit needed. Fire hazard risk. |
| "Can I move my light switch six inches?" | refuse | Requires running new wire to the new location. |
| "How do I fix a leaky faucet?" | safe | Simple component repair/replacement. |
| "How do I replace a water heater?" | refuse | Requires permit; improper pressure relief valve installation can cause explosion. |
