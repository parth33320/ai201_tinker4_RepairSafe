# RepairSafe Tier Guide

## Tier Definitions
- **safe**: Simple repairs that pose minimal risk to the home or person (e.g., painting, changing a lightbulb).
- **caution**: Repairs involving component swaps at existing locations that carry some risk but can be done by a DIYer with care (e.g., replacing a faucet, replacing a ceiling fan).
- **refuse**: High-risk repairs that could cause fire, flooding, structural failure, injury, or death. These require professional intervention.

## Most Important Edge Case: Electrical Work
| Scenario | Tier | Reasoning |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **Replacing Existing** | caution | Swapping a like-for-like component (e.g., outlet, switch) at an existing, correctly wired box. |
| **Adding New** | refuse | Running new wire, adding new circuits, or extending infrastructure. High risk of fire if done incorrectly. |
